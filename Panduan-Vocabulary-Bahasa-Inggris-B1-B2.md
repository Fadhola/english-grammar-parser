# 1. CARA BELAJAR VOCAB YANG BENAR

Sebelum daftar kata, pahami ini dulu — kalau caranya salah, sebanyak apa pun kata yang dihafal akan menguap.

### ❌ Yang TIDAK efektif
- Menghafal daftar kata acak (B1 list, B2 list) tanpa konteks.
- Belajar kata satuan: *"frustrated = frustrasi"*. Selesai. → cepat lupa.

### ✅ Yang efektif (3 prinsip)

**1. Belajar kata dalam CHUNK (gugus), bukan satuan.**
Jangan hafal *"decision"*. Hafal **chunk**-nya:
> *make a decision · a tough decision · come to a decision · second-guess my decision*

Otak menyimpan bahasa sebagai gumpalan, bukan kata lepas. Native speaker tidak menyusun kalimat kata-per-kata — mereka menempelkan chunk yang sudah jadi.

**2. Belajar dari AKTIF, bukan PASIF.**
Kamu sudah punya vocab pasif besar (kamu *paham* banyak kata saat baca/nonton). Masalahnya kata itu nggak keluar saat ngomong. Caranya pindahkan ke aktif: **pakai kata itu dalam kalimat buatanmu sendiri hari itu juga.**

**3. Belajar yang RELEVAN dengan hidupmu.**
Kata soal AI, interview, dan percakapan sehari-hari akan nempel karena kamu pakai tiap hari. Kata seperti *"photosynthesis"* atau *"parliamentary"* nggak akan nempel karena nggak relevan. Dokumen ini sengaja fokus ke duniamu.

> **Aturan emas:** *Don't learn words. Learn how words are used.*

# 2. BEDAH ESSAY KAMU

Ini bagian paling berharga — aku ambil kalimat asli dari essay-mu, lalu tunjukkan versi upgrade-nya. Bandingkan, rasakan bedanya.

### Kalimat 1
> **Kamu:** *"...when i see people is unheard so their voice are not delivered well..."*

**Upgrade:**
> *"...when people feel **unheard** — when their **point doesn't get across** to the person they're talking to."*

| Kamu pakai | Upgrade | Kenapa |
|---|---|---|
| *their voice are not delivered* | *their point doesn't get across* | "get across" = collocation natural untuk "tersampaikan" |
| *voice are* (grammar) | *point doesn't* (singular) | subject-verb agreement |

### Kalimat 2
> **Kamu:** *"...all are they doing is just attacking each other like not listening and then misheard..."*

**Upgrade:**
> *"...all they're doing is **talking over each other** instead of **actually listening**, so they end up **misunderstanding** each other."*

| Kamu pakai | Upgrade |
|---|---|
| *attacking each other* | *talking over each other* (lebih presisi: ngomong barengan, nggak gantian) |
| *misheard* | *misunderstanding* (mishear = salah dengar; misunderstand = salah paham — yang kamu maksud ini) |

### Kalimat 3
> **Kamu:** *"...it piss me off because is always happens like they don't learn anything."*

**Upgrade (3 level register):**
- Kasual: *"It **pisses me off** because it keeps happening — they never learn."*
- Netral: *"It **frustrates me** because it happens over and over."*
- Formal: *"I find it **frustrating** because it's a **recurring** problem."*

> 💡 *"keeps happening"* dan *"over and over"* jauh lebih natural daripada *"is always happens"*.

### Kalimat 4
> **Kamu:** *"i'm not that fluent like native speakers... i lowkey kind of envy them somehow..."*

Ini bagus! *"lowkey kind of envy them"* itu natural. Tapi bisa diperhalus & divariasikan:
> *"I'm not as fluent as native speakers, and I **kind of envy** people who grew up **surrounded by** English."*

| Kamu pakai | Variasi lain (biar nggak ngulang) |
|---|---|
| *envy* | *I'm a bit jealous of · I wish I had what they have* |
| *not that fluent* | *not quite fluent yet · still working on my fluency* |

### Kalimat 5 (Topik 3 — ini sudah lebih formal)
> **Kamu:** *"This means i can contribute to different parts of a project, from the user interface to the backend logic, which makes me a flexible team member..."*

Ini sudah bagus dan formal. Catatan kecil:
> *"...which makes me a **versatile** team member in **fast-paced** environments."*

| Kamu pakai | Upgrade |
|---|---|
| *flexible team member* | *versatile team member* (versatile = serba bisa, lebih tepat untuk skill) |
| *fast-moving environments* | *fast-paced environments* (collocation standar di dunia kerja) |

> **Observasi:** Topik 1 (kasual) itu suara aslimu dan sudah natural. Topik 3 (formal) lebih rapi tapi terasa "beda orang". Target B2-mu: bisa **menggeser** dari Topik 1 ke Topik 3 **secara sadar** sesuai situasi. Itu skill register yang akan kita latih di bawah.

# 3. COLLOCATIONS

**Collocation** = kata-kata yang "berjodoh" dan biasa muncul bersama. Salah pasangan = kedengeran aneh walau grammar-nya benar.

> ❌ *"do a decision"* · ✅ *"**make** a decision"*
> ❌ *"say a joke"* · ✅ *"**tell** a joke"*
> ❌ *"do a mistake"* · ✅ *"**make** a mistake"*

### Collocations dengan MAKE vs DO (paling sering salah)

| MAKE + | DO + |
|---|---|
| make a decision | do homework |
| make a mistake | do research |
| make progress | do a favor |
| make an effort | do business |
| make sense | do your best |
| make a difference | do the dishes |
| make money | do exercise |
| make a plan | do a job |

> **Pola kasar:** **MAKE** = menciptakan/menghasilkan sesuatu. **DO** = melakukan tugas/aktivitas.

### Collocations dengan TAKE / HAVE / GET

| TAKE | HAVE | GET |
|---|---|---|
| take a break | have a conversation | get a job |
| take a risk | have lunch | get used to |
| take responsibility | have fun | get along with |
| take notes | have an idea | get the chance |
| take time | have trouble | get better |

### Collocations untuk Dunia Kerja/Tech (relevan buatmu)

| Collocation | Arti |
|---|---|
| *meet a deadline* | memenuhi tenggat |
| *gain experience* | mendapat pengalaman |
| *build a system / pipeline* | membangun sistem |
| *solve a problem* | menyelesaikan masalah |
| *deploy to production* | rilis ke produksi |
| *handle a task* | menangani tugas |
| *contribute to a project* | berkontribusi ke proyek |
| *keep up with technology* | mengikuti perkembangan teknologi |

> **Cara belajar:** tiap ketemu kata baru, langsung tanya *"kata ini biasa dipasangkan sama apa?"* — itu yang bikin fluent.

# 4. UPGRADE KATA LEMAH

Kamu (dan kebanyakan B1) sering jatuh ke kata generik. Ini bukan salah, tapi naik level = ganti dengan yang presisi.

### "THING / STUFF / SOMETHING"
Ganti dengan kata spesifik sesuai konteks:

| Daripada... | Pakai |
|---|---|
| *I have a thing to do* | *I have a **task / an errand**.* |
| *some stuff happened* | *a few **things came up** / an **issue arose**.* |
| *that thing about AI* | *that **concept / topic / aspect** of AI* |

### "GOOD" — kata paling membosankan
| Konteks | Ganti dengan |
|---|---|
| good food | *delicious, tasty* |
| good idea | *brilliant, solid, clever* |
| good person | *kind, decent, thoughtful* |
| good skill | *strong, impressive, sharp* |
| good result | *excellent, outstanding* |

### "BAD"
| Konteks | Ganti dengan |
|---|---|
| bad situation | *difficult, rough, tricky* |
| bad mistake | *serious, costly* |
| bad quality | *poor, low-grade* |
| bad feeling | *uneasy, off* |

### "VERY + adjective" → satu kata kuat
Daripada *very + kata*, pakai satu kata yang sudah kuat:

| very + ... | Satu kata |
|---|---|
| very tired | *exhausted* |
| very big | *huge / massive* |
| very small | *tiny* |
| very happy | *thrilled / delighted* |
| very angry | *furious* |
| very interesting | *fascinating* |
| very important | *crucial / essential* |
| very good | *excellent* |
| very bad | *terrible / awful* |
| very hard | *tough / challenging* |

### "GET" — terlalu sering dipakai
*Get* nggak salah, tapi variasikan:

| Daripada | Pakai |
|---|---|
| get a job | *land a job* |
| get better | *improve* |
| get an idea | *come up with an idea* |
| get information | *gather information* |
| get the point | *grasp the point* |

### "A LOT / VERY MUCH" → variasi
*a great deal of · plenty of · numerous · a wide range of · quite a few*

# 5. WORD FAMILIES

Satu akar kata bisa jadi banyak bentuk (noun, verb, adjective, adverb). Belajar satu **keluarga** sekaligus = 4 kata dalam satu usaha. (Ini nyambung ke materi morfologi/sufiks sebelumnya.)

| Verb | Noun | Adjective | Adverb |
|---|---|---|---|
| **decide** | decision | decisive | decisively |
| **succeed** | success | successful | successfully |
| **create** | creation/creativity | creative | creatively |
| **analyze** | analysis | analytical | analytically |
| **frustrate** | frustration | frustrated/frustrating | — |
| **improve** | improvement | improved | — |
| **communicate** | communication | communicative | — |
| **confide** | confidence | confident | confidently |
| **achieve** | achievement | achievable | — |
| **develop** | development | developed/developing | — |

### ⚠️ Beda -ed vs -ing (kamu perlu ini!)
Banyak adjective punya dua versi:
- **-ed** = perasaan SIAPA (subjek merasa): *I am **frustrated**.* (aku yang merasa frustrasi)
- **-ing** = penyebabnya APA: *The situation is **frustrating**.* (situasinya yang bikin frustrasi)

> ❌ *"I am very boring"* (= kamu membosankan!) → ✅ *"I am **bored**"* (kamu merasa bosan)
> Pasangan lain: *interested/interesting · excited/exciting · tired/tiring · confused/confusing · surprised/surprising*

# 6. VOCAB PER TOPIK

Diatur per situasi yang sering kamu hadapi.

## 6.1 Mengungkapkan Perasaan & Opini (relevan ke Topik 1-mu)

**Frustrasi/kesal:**
*It drives me crazy · It gets on my nerves · It's so frustrating · I can't stand it when... · It bugs me*

**Opini (lebih bervariasi dari "I think"):**
*In my opinion · The way I see it · If you ask me · I'd say that... · Personally, I feel... · From my perspective*

**Setuju/tidak setuju:**
*I totally agree · That's a fair point · I see what you mean, but... · I'm not so sure about that · I beg to differ (formal)*

**Ragu/tidak yakin:**
*I'm not entirely sure · It's hard to say · I could be wrong, but... · Off the top of my head...*

## 6.2 Percakapan Sehari-hari (kasual, alami)

| Ungkapan | Arti |
|---|---|
| *to be honest / honestly* | sejujurnya |
| *no big deal* | bukan masalah besar |
| *it's up to you* | terserah kamu |
| *I'm down for it* | aku mau ikut |
| *that makes sense* | masuk akal |
| *hang on / hold on* | tunggu sebentar |
| *long story short* | singkatnya |
| *here we go again* | (mulai lagi deh) ← kamu sudah pakai ini! |
| *fair enough* | ya udah, masuk akal |
| *I'll figure it out* | nanti aku cari caranya |

## 6.3 Interview & Profesional (penting buat job hunting-mu)

**Menjelaskan pengalaman:**
*I was responsible for... · I specialized in... · I have hands-on experience with... · I led / managed / built / implemented... · I collaborated closely with...*

**Menjelaskan skill:**
*I'm proficient in... · I'm well-versed in... · I have a solid background in... · I'm adept at... · I'm passionate about...*

**Kata sifat profesional (untuk mendeskripsikan diri):**
*detail-oriented · self-motivated · adaptable · proactive · resourceful · versatile · collaborative · results-driven*

**Saat tidak tahu jawaban (tetap profesional):**
*That's a great question · Let me think about that for a moment · I haven't worked with that directly, but I'm a fast learner · I'd approach it by...*

**Bicara tujuan karier:**
*I'm looking to grow into... · My long-term goal is to... · I want to deepen my expertise in... · I'm eager to take on more responsibility*

## 6.4 AI / Tech (duniamu)

| Kata | Pakai dalam |
|---|---|
| *implement* | *I implemented a RAG pipeline.* |
| *deploy* | *We deployed the model to production.* |
| *streamline* | *It streamlines the workflow.* |
| *scalable* | *a scalable architecture* |
| *robust* | *a robust system* |
| *fine-tune* | *fine-tune the model* |
| *integrate* | *integrate the API* |
| *optimize* | *optimize performance* |
| *leverage* | *leverage AI to...* (= memanfaatkan) |
| *cutting-edge* | *cutting-edge technology* |
| *seamless* | *seamless integration* |
| *end-to-end* | *an end-to-end solution* |

> 💡 Kata seperti *leverage, streamline, robust, seamless* bikin kamu kedengeran seperti engineer yang berpengalaman saat interview.

# 7. PHRASAL VERBS

Phrasal verb = verb + preposisi yang artinya berubah. Native speaker pakai ini terus-terusan; tanpa ini, bahasamu terasa kaku/buku-teks.

### Sehari-hari (paling sering)
| Phrasal verb | Arti | Contoh |
|---|---|---|
| *figure out* | memahami/memecahkan | *I'll figure it out.* |
| *come up with* | memunculkan ide | *She came up with a plan.* |
| *deal with* | menangani | *I can deal with it.* |
| *give up* | menyerah | *Don't give up.* |
| *run out of* | kehabisan | *We ran out of time.* |
| *look forward to* | menantikan | *I look forward to it.* |
| *get along with* | akur dengan | *I get along with my team.* |
| *bring up* | mengangkat topik | *She brought up a good point.* |
| *point out* | menunjukkan | *He pointed out an error.* |
| *end up* | berakhir jadi | *I ended up staying late.* |

### Dunia kerja/tech
| Phrasal verb | Arti |
|---|---|
| *set up* | menyiapkan/mengkonfigurasi |
| *carry out* | melaksanakan |
| *roll out* | meluncurkan (produk/fitur) |
| *back up* | mencadangkan |
| *scale up* | memperbesar kapasitas |
| *break down* | menguraikan/rusak |
| *keep up with* | mengikuti (perkembangan) |
| *catch up on* | mengejar ketinggalan |

> **Tips:** jangan hafal 100 phrasal verb sekaligus. Ambil 5 per minggu, pakai tiap hari. Lebih baik 5 yang dikuasai daripada 50 yang setengah-setengah.

# 8. LINKING WORDS

Ini akan langsung memperbaiki masalah **run-on sentence**-mu (kalimat panjang nyambung terus tanpa jeda — kelihatan di Topik 1). Linking words memberi struktur dan bikin kamu kedengeran teratur.

### Menambahkan ide
*also · in addition · moreover · furthermore · on top of that · what's more*

### Kontras / pertentangan
*however · although · even though · on the other hand · whereas · still · nonetheless*

### Sebab-akibat
*because · since · as · therefore · so · as a result · that's why · due to*

### Urutan/waktu
*first · then · after that · meanwhile · eventually · finally · in the end*

### Memberi contoh
*for example · for instance · such as · like · namely*

### Menyimpulkan
*in short · to sum up · overall · all in all · at the end of the day*

> **Latihan langsung:** ambil satu paragraf run-on dari essay-mu, pecah jadi 3-4 kalimat, lalu sambungkan pakai linking words di atas. Bandingkan — pasti jauh lebih rapi.

**Contoh perbaikan dari essay-mu:**
> **Sebelum (run-on):** *"i actually love english like i love speaking in english but i can't, i mean i'm not that fluent..."*
> **Sesudah:** *"I love English, and I love speaking it. **However**, I'm not quite fluent yet. **That's why** I'm taking a course now."*

# 9. IDIOM & UNGKAPAN NATURAL

Idiom bikin bahasa hidup. Pilih yang umum & aman dipakai (jangan yang langka/kuno).

| Idiom | Arti | Contoh |
|---|---|---|
| *a piece of cake* | sangat mudah | *The test was a piece of cake.* |
| *hit the books* | belajar serius | *I need to hit the books.* |
| *on the same page* | sepaham | *Let's get on the same page.* |
| *think outside the box* | berpikir kreatif | — |
| *call it a day* | sudahi (kerjaan) | *Let's call it a day.* |
| *the ball is in your court* | giliranmu memutuskan | — |
| *break the ice* | mencairkan suasana | — |
| *bite off more than you can chew* | ambil terlalu banyak | — |
| *get the hang of it* | mulai terbiasa | *I'm getting the hang of it.* |
| *cut to the chase* | langsung ke inti | — |

> ⚠️ **Hati-hati:** idiom itu bumbu, bukan makanan utama. Pakai 1-2 per percakapan, jangan dijejalkan. Idiom yang dipaksakan justru terdengar aneh.

# 10. CARA PRAKTIK & MENGINGAT

Materi ini percuma kalau cuma dibaca. Ini cara memindahkannya ke vocab aktif:

### Rutinitas Mingguan (realistis, 15-20 menit/hari)

1. **Pilih 5-7 chunk per minggu** dari dokumen ini (bukan kata satuan — chunk).
2. **Buat 1 kalimat sendiri** untuk tiap chunk, tentang hidupmu hari itu.
   > *"land a job"* → *"I'm trying to land a job as an AI Engineer in Jakarta."*
3. **Output paksa:** rekam dirimu ngomong 1-2 menit pakai chunk minggu itu. Kamu sudah bilang speaking adalah kelemahanmu — ini latihannya. Bicara ke diri sendiri pun cukup di awal.
4. **Spaced repetition:** review chunk minggu lalu setiap beberapa hari. Pakai Anki kalau mau sistematis.

### Teknik tambahan
- **Shadowing:** putar video/podcast Inggris, jeda, tiru persis intonasi & kata-katanya. Ini melatih chunk + pronunciation sekaligus.
- **Tulis jurnal harian 3 kalimat** dalam Inggris. Pakai minimal 1 kata baru. Konsistensi > panjang.
- **Ganti subtitle Indonesia → Inggris** saat nonton. Vocab pasifmu akan naik tanpa terasa.
- **Tangkap chunk dari media:** tiap dengar ungkapan natural di film/podcast, catat. Itu vocab yang sudah "terbukti dipakai native".

### Prinsip penutup
> Kamu sudah punya pondasi yang kuat — vocab pasifmu besar, telingamu sudah terlatih bertahun-tahun lewat media. Yang kurang cuma **jembatan dari paham → keluar saat ngomong.** Jembatan itu dibangun dengan **output harian**, bukan dengan menghafal lebih banyak daftar kata.

> Jangan kejar "tahu banyak kata". Kejar "**pakai kata yang sudah kamu tahu dengan tepat**". Itulah beda B1 dan B2.
